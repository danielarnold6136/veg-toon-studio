🖼️ Story-Scene Thumbnails · MAX CTR

The thumbnail style that actually wins

We restudied the current top-performing sabji-cartoon thumbnails. They all do the same thing — and it's not what our old prompts made. This page is the teardown, the formula, and a ready-to-paste story-scene prompt for every one of our videos.

The pivot, in one line

❌ Our old thumbnails

  • ONE giant crying face filling half the frame
  • Empty space reserved for big Hindi text
  • You can't tell what the story is — just "someone is sad"
  • Looks like a stock emotion, not a drama

✅ What the winners do

  • A full staged scene — 3–4 characters caught mid-drama
  • The whole story is readable in one frozen beat
  • Poor victim on one side, rich betrayer on the other
  • No text at all — the scene creates the curiosity
Why this gets the click: a single sad face asks nothing. A staged scene — a groom crying while his bride walks off with a rich man toward a luxury car — makes your brain finish the story and ask "wait, what happened?!" That curiosity gap is the entire job of a thumbnail, and it's why these are pulling millions of views.

The formula — 8 things every winning thumbnail does

1
Scene, not portrait. 3–4 characters staged in a real place, caught mid-drama. Never a lone floating face.
2
The whole story in one frozen beat. A viewer should understand the entire conflict — who's poor, who's rich, who betrayed whom — without reading a single word.
3
Poor-vs-rich staged left↔right. The victim (crying, ragged) on one side; the betrayer(s) (rich, glamorous, laughing/pointing/embracing) on the other. That juxtaposition IS the hook.
4
One clear emotional micro-action. Streaming tears, a hand reaching out, a mocking point, a secret embrace being witnessed, a hand raised in rejection. Big, exaggerated, readable.
5
Wealth props vs poverty props. Luxury car, designer handbag, gold chains, power-suit, lehenga, glowing restaurant — versus torn rags, patched sack, barefoot, rickety cart, mud. Show the money.
6
No text overlay. Let the scene talk. (Diegetic prop signs like a cart label are fine — that's part of the world.)
7
Mood-matched cinematic lighting. Bright day for a confrontation, warm sunset for a romance-betrayal, dark night for humiliation, rainy grey for tragedy. High saturation, sharp faces, shallow depth of field.
8
Reads at 120px. Big clear silhouettes, faces large enough to see the emotion, strong tonal separation between the two sides. Check it thumbnail-sized before you ship.

🔒 Character Lock — stop the AI swapping the vegetable

The single biggest render bug: the AI silently changes a character's vegetable. In Story 7 our karela saas rendered as a baingan, and our mirchi jethani rendered as a tomato. It happens because rare, odd-shaped vegetables get swapped for the common, easy "character vegetables" the model draws best. Every thumbnail character must now be locked so this can't happen.

The three rules

  1. Define each character in a fixed block: SPECIES (say it in capitals and say "must be a …"), then SHAPE & SKIN, then FACE NOW (the only part that changes per scene), then DRESS, then DO NOT.
  2. Add a negative — name the wrong vegetable. "She is a KARELA — NOT an eggplant/brinjal/baingan, no purple body." Naming the exact wrong swap is what blocks it.
  3. Kill colour cues that pull toward the wrong veg. A green karela in a maroon/purple pallu renders as an eggplant, because the model fuses the purple cloth into the body. We recoloured her pallu to mustard-gold. Never dress a green vegetable in purple.

Worked example — the locked character block

CHARACTER LOCK · KARELAMMA
KARELAMMA (strict saas) — she is a KARELA / BITTER GOURD and must be a bitter gourd. SHAPE & SKIN: a long tapered spindle body entirely covered in raised knobbly warty ridges and bumpy lumps running lengthwise, deep forest-green with pale-green ridge highlights, a grey leafy top-knot bun. FACE NOW: stern downturned scowl, sharp slanted eyes, one bumpy arm pointing. DRESS (fixed): a heavy MUSTARD-GOLD and ochre pallu with gold zari border (NOT maroon, NOT purple), big gold nose-ring, gold bangles. DO NOT: she is NOT an eggplant, brinjal or baingan — no purple skin, no smooth teardrop body; keep her dark-green and warty.

Already applied: all 26 thumbnail prompts across Stories 1–10 now use this locked-character format. The scenes, heroes, emotions and props stay different from each other — only the way each character is defined is now this strict. Reuse the same lock block for that character in any new render, and render 2–3 takes and pick the clean one.

The one rule about text

Default: no text

Every winning thumbnail above is text-free. The scene does the selling. Ship it with no words — this is the default, and it also dodges the AI's broken Devanagari rendering completely.

If you must add words

Add them afterwards in Canva, never in the AI prompt. Keep it to 2–4 huge words in the bottom or a top corner, in Mukta / Baloo 2 ExtraBold with a thick dark outline, kept clear of every face. One emotional word beats a sentence — e.g. धोखा 💔, झूठा इलज़ाम 😭, बेइज़्ज़ती 😱.

Everything else from before still holds: render-lock always (anthropomorphic veg, no humans, no AI text), generate at 1280×720, and eyeball it at thumbnail size before you ship.

🎵 Music that does the emotional heavy-lifting

BGM Guide — score the emotion, cheaply

With no narrator, the music now carries the feeling the voice-over used to. Get this right and a 6-minute veg-drama makes people cry, cheer and stay. Here is exactly what to use, where to get it free (and monetization-safe), and how to keep every channel's sound unique — on a tiny budget.

Is BGM important? (Yes — it is the emotion)

In this niche the plot is simple; the feeling is the product. Betrayal lands because the strings ache; the comeback thrills because the dholak kicks in. Since we dropped the narrator, BGM + dialogue are the only two emotional channels left — so music is not optional, it is half the video.

Where to get it — free & monetization-safe

⚠️ Do NOT build on NCS (NoCopyrightSounds). NCS registers its tracks in YouTube Content ID and will claim the ad revenue on any video that uses them — you keep the views but lose the money. "No copyright" ≠ "free to monetize". Skip NCS for our channels.

🟢 Tier A — Free baseline (use these)

  • YouTube Audio Library (in Creator Studio) — pre-cleared, keep 100% revenue, most tracks need no attribution. Filter by mood (sad/dramatic/inspirational) + cinematic. Your default.
  • Pixabay Music — free, commercial, no attribution; has "bollywood sad", "Indian", "emotional" tracks. Great for desi flute/sarangi feels.
  • Chosic (free Indian section), Mixkit, Musopen (public-domain classical).

🇮🇳 Desi emotional sound (the "cry" factor)

  • Pixabay → search "bollywood sad", "sarangi", "Indian emotional".
  • Chosic → free Indian instrumental.
  • Fesliyan Studios → emotional piano/violin/duduk (free w/ credit).
  • TunePocket / Storyblocks → authentic sarangi/shehnai (paid/sub) if you want premium.

AI music — a unique sound per channel (the farm angle)

Running 5–6 separate channels? If they all reuse the same free tracks, that shared audio becomes a fingerprint that helps link them. AI-generated BGM gives every channel its own audio so no two channels share a single track — for a few dollars a month total.

💸 Cheapest that fit us

  • Mubert — Creator ~$11.69/mo: every track commercial-cleared, flat fee, has an API → automate per-channel cue libraries in bulk. Best for pure background loops at scale.
  • Suno — Pro ~$10/mo: commercial license while subscribed; great for custom emotional cues. Best value/quality.
  • Soundraw (~$20+/mo): mood+length controls, fully royalty-free (pricier).

Plan: ONE shared Suno/Mubert sub (~$10–12/mo) makes unique cue packs for ALL channels — pennies per video. Keep YouTube Audio Library as the free fallback.

Treblo (ex-Sonauto) — free, step-by-step for BGM

Sonauto.ai is now Treblo (treblo.com) — a free AI music generator that makes full tracks and can export stems. Its v3 engine does long tracks (~4.5 min), so it's great for generating a unique cue per channel at zero cost.

⚙️ Exact settings for clean BGM

  • Description / prompt: the mood + instruments + tempo (no story). See the copy-prompts on the right.
  • Instrumental: turn it ON — we want NO vocals under the dialogue.
  • Lyrics box: leave it empty.
  • Prompt strength / adherence: set high so it follows your description closely.
  • Duration: match your scene/section length (loopable cues of 30–60s are easiest to edit).
  • Seed: change the seed per channel → each channel gets a unique track (kills the shared-audio fingerprint).
  • Export: download the track; if any vocals slipped in, export the instrumental stem only.
Treblo says the rights to what you make are yours with no usage restriction, but its commercial terms are loosely worded. So keep YouTube Audio Library / Pixabay as the monetization-safe baseline, use Treblo for free unique cues, and keep your generation records.

How to add BGM (cheap & in bulk)

  • Build a cue library per channel — ~8 loops (heartbreak, lonely, tension, spark, rise, triumph, warm-resolution, neutral). Reuse within a channel, keep unique across channels.
  • Follow the per-scene BGM cues on each script page; drop the matching loop under each beat.
  • Duck under dialogue — lower the music ~8–10 dB when a character speaks, swell between lines. Keep music ≈ −18 to −22 LUFS so the engine-voiced lines stay clear.
  • Add free SFX — whoosh on transitions, a "ding" on the idea moment, a sad violin sting on the betrayal (YouTube Audio Library / Pixabay SFX).
  • One emotional climax — let the music drop to near-silence right before the twist, then hit hard. That silence-then-swell is the whole trick.

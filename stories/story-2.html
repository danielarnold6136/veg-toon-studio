In a drought-cracked sabzi-mandi village, a poor, mocked pumpkin named Kundan keeps inventing humble jugaad water-machines while a flashy rich capsicum, Shanu, sabotages every attempt and flaunts an imported gadget — until the heat nearly kills a child and an elder, Shanu's imported cooler explodes in public, and Kundan's biggest jugaad saves the entire village, winning back the heart of the carrot he loves and teaching that honest effort beats shiny show-off.

Overview

🎣 The hook

Cold-open on the worst public moment: Kundan's water-machine is smashed and he is splashed with mud and laughed at by the whole mandi while Shanu poses with his imported gadget — freeze on Kundan's tear-filled eye and a child fainting in the background, then hard-cut to "3 din pehle" to rewind. The shock + an unanswered question (who smashed it? will the child live?) forces the viewer to keep watching.

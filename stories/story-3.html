Tara, a poor but honest tomato in love with a peas-girl, is framed for a gold-theft by a jealous rich onion landlord and publicly disgraced and driven out of the sabzi-mandi; years later he returns transformed and prosperous and, at a packed mandi festival, exposes the onion's own fraud — clearing his name as karma lands and the betrayer who once mocked him begs forgiveness.

Overview

🎣 The hook

Cold-open on the PEAK humiliation, not the calm: a rope tightens around a sobbing tomato's wrists as the whole mandi screams "चोर! चोर!" and a fat gold-draped onion smirks holding a stolen necklace — then hard-cut to "...लेकिन इस चोरी की असली कहानी कुछ और थी" via on-screen action, and rewind. The viewer instantly needs to know: who really stole it, and does the tomato ever clear his name? The injustice + the smirking rich villain + the crying lover create the irresistible click. Every scene ends on a micro-hook (a dropped clue, a threat, a tear, a closing fist) so no one can scroll away.

Full Hindi Script (paste into Veo 3)

The entire story as one clean Hindi screenplay — every scene & dialogue, formatted. Copy it whole.

📈 Why it holds attention: Tension is engineered to climb every single scene and never sag. Act 1 opens on the maximum-shame freeze-frame (scene 1), rewinds to a warm low-stakes love/dream (2-4), then the rival's threat (5-6) and the public framing/disgrace (7-9) drive stakes up fast. Act 2 hits rock bottom and deepens it twice — mockery, then his lover seemingly lost (10-13) — before the smallest flicker of hope, immediately undercut by a setback (14-16). The climb is never smooth: every win (17, 20, 23) is answered by escalating sabotage from the onion (rumor 18, blockade/poison-rumor 21, a hired frame 24) that creates a fresh, lower low (19, 22, 25 false-defeat) so the audience keeps fearing he'll lose. The reveal is staged in rising layers — a hidden witness (26), a ledger (27), the real necklace (28) — peaking at the packed festival showdown (29-31) with the highest public stakes. Karma lands hard and visibly (32), the betrayer's regret is earned and tearful (33), and only then the hero speaks the moral on an uplift (34). No filler beats: each scene introduces a new threat, clue, ally, or reversal and ends on a micro-hook (a tear, a smirk, a dropped coin, a closing fist, a whispered threat).

Character Design Sheet

Lock these looks before generating anything. Each design is written to be redrawn identically in every scene.

🦸
तारा (टमाटर) (Tara (Tomato))
hero

Personality: Poor, honest, hardworking and warm-hearted; gentle but unbreakable in his integrity. Humiliated yet never bitter — he answers injustice with patience, cleverness and relentless effort, transforming from a penniless cart-vendor into a dignified, prosperous pickle-maker.

Look: Glossy round bright-red tomato with a small green leaf-crown stalk, huge expressive eyes with strong catch-lights, tiny stubby arms; early scenes a patched dusty kurta and frayed cloth; later a clean crisp cream kurta with a modest waistcoat, standing taller and confident. Soft Pixar rim-light.

Voice: Warm, soft mid-tenor; sincere and steady. Trembles with restrained emotion in despair, never shrill; grows firmer, calmer and more commanding as he rises — final moral line is slow, resonant and resolute.

🦸
मटर (मटर-कन्या) (Matar (Peas-girl))
heroine / love interest

Personality: Loyal, gentle and quietly fierce in her loyalty; believes in Tara's innocence when the whole mandi turns against him. Tender but brave, she waits faithfully through the years.

Look: A bright-green rounded pea-pod girl with three little peas peeking like a crown, big dewy eyes, long lashes, a small marigold tucked at her side; flowing light-green dupatta. Soft blush cheeks, delicate stubby hands.

Voice: Sweet, soft soprano; emotional and earnest. Cracks into sobs during the framing and farewell, glows with warm relief at the reunion.

🥕
सेठ प्याज़ (Seth Pyaaz (Onion Landlord))
villain

Personality: Rich, arrogant, jealous and cruel; obsessed with status, money and possessing what isn't his. Stages the theft out of envy and lust for Matar, then sabotages Tara again and again — a coward beneath the gold, undone by his own fraud.

Look: A large glossy purple-and-white onion with layered papery skin folds suggesting jowls, draped in heavy gold chains and rings, a smug curled mouth and beady narrowed eyes; rich maroon embroidered vest, fat fingers. Oily sheen, looming posture.

Voice: Deep, oily, booming baritone with a sneering laugh; mocking and grand when powerful, cracking into a high panicked stammer and grovel when his fraud is exposed.

🥕
चमचा मिर्च (Chamcha Mirch (Chilli Henchman))
sidekick villain / betrayer who regrets

Personality: The onion's greedy, sycophantic green-chilli henchman who plants the necklace and spreads the rumors for money; ultimately cornered, exposed, and tearfully repentant.

Look: A skinny long green chilli with a curled pointed tip, shifty darting eyes, a thin sly grin, sneaky hunched posture; small dark waistcoat, twitchy stubby hands always rubbing together.

Voice: Thin, nasal, slithery tenor; conspiratorial whisper when scheming, breaking into a blubbering, guilt-stricken cry when he begs forgiveness.

🥕
अदरक बाबा (Adrak Baba (Ginger Elder))
mentor / wise elder

Personality: A wrinkled, kindly old ginger sage; Tara's moral compass and guide who lifts him from rock bottom and teaches him to rebuild through honest skill.

Look: A knobbly tan ginger-root old man with gnarled branching limbs, bushy white eyebrows and a flowing white beard, half-moon spectacles, a wooden walking stick, simple saffron shawl; gentle crinkled smile.

Voice: Old, gravelly, slow and soothing; grandfatherly warmth with measured, proverb-like wisdom.

🥕
जाँच अधिकारी नींबू (Inspector Nimbu (Lemon Officer))
honest authority / quality inspector

Personality: A bright, no-nonsense lemon inspector who tests Tara's pickle fairly and later reads out the damning ledger that exposes the onion's insurance fraud.

Look: A small zesty bright-yellow lemon with a tidy little cap and clipboard, round spectacles, a crisp official sash; alert eyes, brisk upright stance.

Voice: Crisp, clear, official mid-tone; precise and fair, rising with conviction when announcing the verdict.

🥕
मूँगफली बुढ़िया (Moongphali Budhiya (Old Peanut Witness))
hidden witness

Personality: A timid, frightened old peanut who secretly witnessed the original frame-up and was hidden away in fear; finds her courage to testify at the climax.

Look: A tiny wrinkled beige peanut-shell granny in a faded shawl, hunched, with trembling stubby hands, watery wide eyes; a little knitted cap. Frail but determined.

Voice: Frail, quavering, elderly female; starts in a frightened whisper, then steadies into trembling but firm conviction as she names the onion.

🥕
सिपाही करेला (Sipahi Karela (Constable Bitter-gourd))
law / minor authority

Personality: A dutiful bitter-gourd constable who first comes to search Tara but, seeing the proof, arrests the real culprit and seizes the gold.

Look: A bumpy green bitter-gourd constable with a khaki cap and belt, a small baton, stern bushy brow, upright firm posture.

Voice: Gruff, firm, authoritative bass; clipped and official.

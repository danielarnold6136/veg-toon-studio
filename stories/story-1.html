📜 Ready-to-shoot script · ~6.2 min

गरीब करेले से बेवफाई 😭💔 फिर करम ने जो दिखाया... 😱

Kallu, a poor but honest bitter-gourd vegetable-seller, is betrayed on his own wedding day when his beloved grape, Rasili, is lured away by Sheth Anaar, a flashy rich pomegranate who flaunts gold. Publicly humiliated and broken, Kallu rebuilds his life through honesty and a clever desi pickle-business idea, becoming the most respected vegetable in the mandi — until the truth bursts out: the rich pomegranate is literally hollow and rotten inside, his wealth as empty as his shell.

Overview

🎣 The hook

The video opens mid-catastrophe: a wedding mandap, garlands in hand, the whole mandi watching — and the bride literally walking away on the arm of a glittering rich rival while the groom stands frozen, tears welling. We freeze on Kallu's shattered face and pose the "फिर जो हुआ..." curiosity gap, so the viewer must keep watching to learn how a vegetable this humiliated could ever rise. Shock + heartbreak + a karmic promise, all within the first 8 seconds.

🖼️ Thumbnail concept

Split-tension composition: LEFT third — close-up of poor green bitter-gourd Kallu's face streaming with big cartoon tears, a torn marigold garland slipping from his little hand, dull lighting. RIGHT two-thirds — glittering red pomegranate Sheth Anaar in a golden sherwani smugly pulling away a blushing green grape bride, gold coins raining. A glowing crack runs down the pomegranate hinting he is hollow inside. Big bold Hindi overlay text top: "शादी के दिन बेवफाई! 💔" and a small red shock burst bottom-right: "अंदर से खोखला निकला 😱". High contrast, saturated, dramatic.

📝 Title (Hindi)

गरीब करेले से बेवफाई 😭💔 फिर करम ने जो दिखाया... 😱

Betrayal of the Poor Bitter-Gourd — Then What Karma Showed...

🪔 Moral / सीख

सच्चाई और मेहनत कभी खाली नहीं जाती — और जो ऊपर से चमकता है, ज़रूरी नहीं अंदर से भी सच्चा हो।

Distinctness: This story uses a completely fresh cast that avoids the reference channels' signature combos (no Aloo+Mooli+Bhindi, no Aloo+Gajar): the hero is a bitter-gourd (Karela), the love-interest a grape (Angoor), the rival a pomegranate (Anaar), with an original ginger-elder mentor (Adrak Kaka) and a clearly-defined village-crowd ensemble (मंडी की भीड़) as the public voice. The specific conflict is also fresh and concrete — a wedding-mandap betrayal followed by an honest bitter-gourd-pickle business comeback, with the hyped signature twist being the rich pomegranate literally cracking open in the public square to reveal he is hollow, seedless and rotten inside with fake painted coins (a karmic visual unique to a pomegranate's natural seed-filled form, so it lands harder and cannot be copied to other casts). Only the universal emotional formula (poverty vs wealth, love-betrayal, public humiliation, honest comeback, karma, spoken moral) is retained; all character names, designs, accessories (Anaar's money-purse, Rasili's nath, Adrak Kaka's bamboo stick), the pickle-business mechanic, the mentor, the named crowd ensemble, and the hollow-pomegranate reveal are original, keeping it clearly distinct from existing sabzi-cartoon uploads and free of plot/character reuse.
🎵 Music arc: The score arcs from warmth to devastation to triumphant resolve. Open on a single tense villain stinger over the betrayal hook, then warm harmonium/acoustic for the love-story flashback. Collapse into slow sarangi/violin heartbreak at rock-bottom (scenes 4-5), lift into a gentle hopeful harmonium spark (6-7), then build rising dholak/percussion energy through the hard-work montage and success (8-9). Turn ominous and tense for the villain's unraveling (10-11), settle into a calm dignified low strings bed for the confrontation and bittersweet regret (12-13), and finish on a fully uplifting celebratory dholak-and-flute crescendo that lands the moral (14). → BGM Guide

Character Design Sheet

Lock these looks before generating anything. Each design is written to be redrawn identically in every scene.

🦸
कल्लू करेला (Kallu Karela (Bitter-Gourd))
hero

Personality: Poor, honest, soft-hearted and hardworking. Shy in love, easily hurt, but quietly stubborn. Never lies, never begs, carries his dignity even when humiliated. His arc: heartbreak to disciplined grind to respected success.

Look: A medium bitter-gourd (karela) shaped character: bumpy, ridged dark-green glossy skin with lighter green highlights along the bumps, a slightly tapered body, big round shiny black eyes with hopeful eyebrows, small expressive mouth. Tiny stubby arms and short legs. Wears a faded, patched off-white kurta and a thin frayed brown gamcha (cloth) over one shoulder, simple brown chappals. A small sprig of green leaf as a tuft on his head like hair. Honest, gentle face; in sad scenes his green looks duller, in success scenes his skin is brighter and glossier and he wears a clean cream kurta with a marigold-orange waistcoat.

Voice: Warm mid-tenor young-adult male, soft-spoken and earnest with a gentle rural Hindi accent; low-to-mid pitch, slightly breathy in sad scenes, steady and confident with quiet authority in success scenes. Never shouts; power comes from calm sincerity. Consistent honest, grounded texture throughout.

💚
रसीली अंगूर (Rasili Angoor (Grape))
love-interest

Personality: Sweet but impressionable, dazzled by shine and luxury. Not evil — weak. She betrays Kallu out of greed for an easier life, then slowly realizes her mistake. Capable of remorse.

Look: A petite round green grape character (a single large grape with a small green stalk-tuft on top like a topknot), smooth glossy light-green skin with a soft sheen, big curious almond eyes with long lashes, rosy blush cheeks, dainty arms and legs. Wears a bright pink-and-gold lehenga-style outfit with tiny bangles and a small nose-ring (nath). In the wedding scene she wears red bridal attire with a thin gold border and the same small nath. In later regret scenes her clothes are simpler and her eyes look downcast and teary, nath removed or replaced with a tiny plain stud.

Voice: Light, sweet young-adult female, higher pitch with a bright lilt and delicate breathiness; playful and dreamy early on, then thinner, trembling and tearful in regret scenes. Soft feminine timbre, gentle vibrato when crying.

😼
सेठ अनार (Sheth Anaar (Pomegranate))
rival

Personality: Arrogant, flashy, boastful rich pomegranate who flaunts gold and mocks the poor. Loud, theatrical, condescending. THE TWIST: he is literally hollow and rotten inside — all shine, no substance — and his wealth is borrowed/fake.

Look: A large round ruby-red pomegranate character with glossy deep-red crown-like calyx on top (resembling a small golden crown), shiny waxy red skin with subtle facetting, big haughty half-lidded eyes, a thin curled mustache, a smug grin showing one gold tooth. Stubby arms heavy with gold rings and a thick gold chain, plus a fat round money-purse always tied at his belt. Wears a flashy embroidered golden-and-maroon sherwani with a peacock-feather turban brooch. Walks with a pompous strut. In the climax a crack splits his shell to reveal he is HOLLOW and dry-brown inside (no seeds), spilling dust and fake painted coins instead of real fruit.

Voice: Booming, oily middle-aged male baritone, loud and theatrical with an exaggerated showman swagger and a sneering edge; rolls his laughter. Pitch drops to a tense, hissing whisper when hiding his secret and cracks into shrill panic when exposed. Greasy, arrogant texture.

🥕
बूढ़ा अदरक काका (Buddha Adrak Kaka (Old Ginger Uncle))
supporting

Personality: Wise, kind elder of the mandi. A mentor figure who shelters Kallu at rock bottom and sparks his comeback idea with a gentle nudge. Speaks in calm proverbs.

Look: An elderly knobbly ginger-root character, tan-beige bumpy skin with little branching knobs like limbs, kind crinkled eyes behind small round spectacles, white bushy eyebrows and a small white beard-tuft. Slightly hunched, ALWAYS leans on or keeps a thin bamboo walking stick at hand (it is part of his recurring silhouette even when he sets it aside to clap). Wears a simple white dhoti-kurta and a brown shawl, a wooden prayer-bead mala around his neck. Warm, grandfatherly presence.

Voice: Elderly grandfatherly male, low and gravelly with a slow, warm, wise cadence and a slight tremor of age; reassuring and affectionate. Speaks unhurried, like a village elder giving blessings.

🥕
मंडी की भीड़ (Mandi Crowd (Ensemble of Village Vegetables))
supporting

Personality: The collective voice of the sabzi-mandi villagers — ordinary small vegetables and fruits who gossip, gasp, judge and ultimately cheer. They embody public opinion: they witness Kallu's humiliation, react in shock at Anaar's exposure, and finally celebrate Kallu's victory. Their reactions amplify each emotional beat.

Look: A varied ensemble of small cute glossy 3D Pixar-style anthropomorphic Indian vegetables and fruits with big expressive eyes and tiny stubby limbs — assorted tomatoes, brinjals (baingan), potatoes, cauliflowers, green chillies, lady-fingers (bhindi) and mangoes — dressed in simple colourful village clothing (plain kurtas, saris, gamchas, small turbans). They appear as a clustered background crowd, faces turning in unison, hands raised, mouths agape in shocked scenes and beaming with raised arms in celebratory scenes. No single one is a named lead; they read clearly as 'the village crowd'.

Voice: Overlapping ensemble of mixed village voices — male and female, varied ages — reacting in unison; gasps, murmurs and excited shouts. Treated as a crowd bed, no single lead, with collective surprise and uproar energy.

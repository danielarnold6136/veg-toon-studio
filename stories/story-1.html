On his wedding day, honest poor bitter-gourd Kallu is abandoned by his beloved grape Rasili, who is lured away by the flashy rich pomegranate Sheth Anaar. As Anaar sabotages Kallu's every attempt to rebuild through an honest pickle business — spreading rumours, buying out suppliers, getting him evicted, and framing him — Kallu, backed by old Ginger Uncle and a loyal friend, rises step by step. At the grand village festival contest, Anaar's shell finally cracks to reveal he is hollow and rotten inside, his wealth fake painted coins and dust, and karma delivers the truth: honesty is the only real wealth.

Overview

🎣 The hook

Cold open on the wedding mandap: the crowd gasps as bride Rasili lets go of Kallu's hand mid-ceremony and steps toward the glittering Sheth Anaar, who tosses gold coins in the air. Kallu's marigold garland slips and falls into the dust in slow motion as his hopeful smile collapses — a single freeze on his tear-filled eyes with the on-screen question 'क्यों?' (Why?), before we rewind. Maximum betrayal shock in the first 10 seconds to stop the scroll.

📈 Why it holds attention: Tension rises every single scene with no sag. The betrayal lands publicly in the cold open, then the rewind deepens our love for Kallu so the loss hurts more. After rock-bottom, each comeback win is immediately answered by a BIGGER Anaar sabotage: rumour to poison the brand, then buying out his chilli/mango suppliers, then bribing the landlord to evict him, then planting rotten stock to frame him for cheating, then rigging the festival contest judges. Each setback is a false-defeat that Kallu overcomes with a cleverer, more public honest move, pulling more of the crowd to his side while Anaar visibly panics and overreaches. Stakes climb from personal (a girl) to social (his name/izzat) to public (the whole mandi's trust) to the grand festival climax watched by everyone — where the highest stakes meet the hollowness reveal. The crack-open of Anaar is the literal and emotional peak, immediately followed by karma, Rasili's regret, and Kallu's quiet moral on top.

Character Design Sheet

🦸
कल्लू करेला (Kallu Karela (Bitter-Gourd))
hero

Personality: Honest, gentle and earnest; quietly resilient. Believes integrity is wealth and never lowers himself to revenge. Soft-spoken but unbreakable; his power is calm sincerity, not anger. Grows from heartbroken and humiliated to dignified and confident without ever becoming arrogant.

Look: A medium bitter-gourd (karela) shaped character: bumpy, ridged dark-green glossy skin with lighter green highlights along the bumps, a slightly tapered body, big round shiny black eyes with hopeful eyebrows, small expressive mouth. Tiny stubby arms and short legs. Wears a faded, patched off-white kurta and a thin frayed brown gamcha over one shoulder, simple brown chappals. A small sprig of green leaf as a tuft on his head like hair. Honest, gentle face; in sad scenes his green looks duller, in success scenes his skin is brighter and glossier and he wears a clean cream kurta with a marigold-orange waistcoat.

Voice: Warm mid-tenor young-adult male, soft-spoken and earnest with a gentle rural Hindi accent; low-to-mid pitch, slightly breathy in sad scenes, steady and confident with quiet authority in success scenes. Never shouts; power comes from calm sincerity. Consistent honest, grounded texture throughout.

💚
रसीली अंगूर (Rasili Angoor (Grape))
love-interest

Personality: Sweet and dreamy but naive and dazzled by glitter; weak in the moment she chooses wealth over love, which becomes her deepest regret. Her conscience never fully dies — it is she who finally witnesses and exposes Anaar's frame-up, redeeming herself through honesty even at personal cost.

Look: A petite round green grape character (a single large grape with a small green stalk-tuft on top like a topknot), smooth glossy light-green skin with a soft sheen, big curious almond eyes with long lashes, rosy blush cheeks, dainty arms and legs. Wears a bright pink-and-gold lehenga-style outfit with tiny bangles and a small nose-ring (nath). In the wedding scene she wears red bridal attire with a thin gold border and the same small nath. In later regret scenes her clothes are simpler and her eyes look downcast and teary, nath removed or replaced with a tiny plain stud.

Voice: Light, sweet young-adult female, higher pitch with a bright lilt and delicate breathiness; playful and dreamy early on, then thinner, trembling and tearful in regret scenes. Soft feminine timbre, gentle vibrato when crying.

😼
सेठ अनार (Sheth Anaar (Pomegranate))
rival

Personality: Arrogant, greedy and theatrical; flaunts wealth to dominate and buys whatever he cannot win. A serial saboteur who escalates from threats to rumours, blockades, eviction and framing. Beneath the swagger he is terrified of his secret — his riches and his very body are fake and hollow — and he cracks into shrill panic the instant he is exposed.

Look: A large round ruby-red pomegranate character with glossy deep-red crown-like calyx on top (resembling a small golden crown), shiny waxy red skin with subtle facetting, big haughty half-lidded eyes, a thin curled mustache, a smug grin showing one gold tooth. Stubby arms heavy with gold rings and a thick gold chain, plus a fat round money-purse always tied at his belt. Wears a flashy embroidered golden-and-maroon sherwani with a peacock-feather turban brooch. Walks with a pompous strut. In the climax a crack splits his shell to reveal he is HOLLOW and dry-brown inside (no seeds), spilling dust and fake painted coins instead of real fruit.

Voice: Booming, oily middle-aged male baritone, loud and theatrical with an exaggerated showman swagger and a sneering edge; rolls his laughter. Pitch drops to a tense, hissing whisper when hiding his secret and cracks into shrill panic when exposed. Greasy, arrogant texture.

🥕
बूढ़ा अदरक काका (Buddha Adrak Kaka (Old Ginger Uncle))
supporting

Personality: Wise, warm grandfatherly mentor and Kallu's moral anchor and loyal ally. Speaks in proverbs and blessings, never panics, and quietly supplies both encouragement and practical help (the starter jar, his own farm's chillies). The steady voice that karma will turn the tables.

Look: An elderly knobbly ginger-root character, tan-beige bumpy skin with little branching knobs like limbs, kind crinkled eyes behind small round spectacles, white bushy eyebrows and a small white beard-tuft. Slightly hunched, ALWAYS leans on or keeps a thin bamboo walking stick at hand (it is part of his recurring silhouette even when he sets it aside to clap). Wears a simple white dhoti-kurta and a brown shawl, a wooden prayer-bead mala around his neck. Warm, grandfatherly presence.

Voice: Elderly grandfatherly male, low and gravelly with a slow, warm, wise cadence and a slight tremor of age; reassuring and affectionate. Speaks unhurried, like a village elder giving blessings.

🥕
मंडी की भीड़ (Mandi Crowd (Ensemble of Village Vegetables))
supporting

Personality: The fickle public conscience of the village — quick to mock, easily swayed by rumour, but ultimately won over by visible honesty. Their turning from jeering to cheering measures Kallu's rise and Anaar's fall; they deliver the people's verdict at the climax.

Look: A varied ensemble of small cute glossy 3D Pixar-style anthropomorphic Indian vegetables and fruits with big expressive eyes and tiny stubby limbs — assorted tomatoes, brinjals (baingan), potatoes, cauliflowers, green chillies, lady-fingers (bhindi) and mangoes — dressed in simple colourful village clothing (plain kurtas, saris, gamchas, small turbans). They appear as a clustered background crowd, faces turning in unison, hands raised, mouths agape in shocked scenes and beaming with raised arms in celebratory scenes. No single one is a named lead; they read clearly as 'the village crowd'.

Voice: Overlapping ensemble of mixed village voices — male and female, varied ages — reacting in unison; gasps, murmurs and excited shouts. Treated as a crowd bed, no single lead, with collective surprise and uproar energy.

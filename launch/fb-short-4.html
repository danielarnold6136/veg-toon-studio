When the family land is split, the greedy cousin grabs the lush green field and dumps the dry, cracked plot on humble Matar, laughing that it is worthless. Matar plants a hardy millet the whole village mocks. Then a merciless dry season kills the 'good' land — and only his tough crop survives, standing gold across the barren plot and making him the richest, wisest farmer in the village.

Why a scroller stops: A young farmer is handed a dead, cracked plot of land while his cousin laughs — and you stay to see what he could possibly grow on it.

Setup: the land is split unfairly→Mockery: 'your land is worthless'→The choice: he sows a hardy millet→The grind: water under a harsh sun→The drought: the good land dies→The survivor: gold on barren earth→The turn: he shares his seed→Payoff: wisdom, not land
Built for continuity — it should play as ONE unbroken film, not clips. Two things do that: (1) the story stays in a few connected sets and the camera flows between beats (walk / pan / push) instead of cutting — each scene's Flow line says how it joins the one before. (2) when you render, take the LAST frame of each finished clip and use it as the input / first image for the next clip (Flow → “Frames to Video” / Extend); every video prompt below also carries a continuity lock telling Veo to continue the previous shot with no cut.

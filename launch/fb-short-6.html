Tamatar, a tomato cart vendor, has saved for months to buy his daughter a laptop for her online exam. A smooth online dealer appears in the market with a half-price offer, takes the whole amount by QR on the spot, and promises delivery by courier. The parcel comes — and it holds a brick. His number is switched off. A young neighbour makes him report it within the first hour, the account is frozen, and every rupee comes back — while the dealer is left sitting in an empty stall.

Why a scroller stops: A father tears open the parcel his whole savings bought — and a plain grey brick falls out onto the road. You stay to find out whether he ever gets that money back.

Hook: the brick falls out of the box→Setup: a father saving for his daughter's exam→The offer: half price, pay now by QR→The payment: the whole tin emptied→The parcel: a brick, and a dead number→Low point: she says she'll manage without it→The turn: report it inside the first hour→Karma: the account freezes, the money returns→Payoff: she sits her exam · check first, pay later
NEW in this pair — the first 4 seconds are a HOOK, not the start of the story. Scene 6.0 is a cold open: it lifts the hardest moment out of the middle of the film (the brick), plays it before anything is explained, and holds on the reaction. The film then rewinds into scene 6.1 and runs forward normally — and when it reaches that moment again in scene 6.4, it plays in full with its consequence. Only ONE join in the whole film is a deliberate cut (6.0 → 6.1, the rewind); every other scene flows on with no cut at all.
Built for continuity — after the hook it should play as ONE unbroken film, not clips. Two things do that: (1) the story stays in a few connected sets and the camera flows between beats (walk / pan / push) instead of cutting — each scene’s Flow line says how it joins the one before. (2) when you render, take the LAST frame of each finished clip and use it as the input / first image for the next clip (Flow → “Frames to Video” / Extend); every video prompt below also carries a continuity lock telling Veo to continue the previous shot with no cut.
Same reference model as Shorts 1–5: whole-vegetable head with NO human hair (the veg’s own top is the crown) · a complete payoff arc · render the reference portraits first and reuse them as Flow Ingredients · every dialogue line spoken inside its own clip (🎤 one mouth moving at a time — no separate VO recording) · neutral/Hindu framing · modern, everyday India.

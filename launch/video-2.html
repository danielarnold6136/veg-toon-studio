Why video 1 got only 5 impressions

First, the honest part: a brand-new channel starts with zero reach. With 0 subscribers there is no Home feed, no subscription feed and no suggested traffic yet — YouTube's own Help says new channels earn impressions almost entirely from search until watch data builds up. Low single-digit impressions on day 1 of video 1 is normal, not a penalty. But our metadata made even search impossible:

What YouTube itself says (so this isn't guesswork)

Every rule on this page is grounded in official YouTube Help documentation, checked 2026-07-08:

  • 5 impressions on day 1 is expected, not a penalty. YouTube ranks your video "against all the other videos a viewer might watch" — not just your own — and recommendations need viewer watch-history that a 0-sub channel doesn't have yet. New channels start with almost pure search reach. (support.google.com/youtube/answer/7628154 · /11914225)
  • Impressions come from the homepage, "Up Next" suggested, search results and subscription feeds — search is the only one open to us right now, which is exactly why the Devanagari + keyword-tail title matters. (answer/7628154)
  • More impressions are earned by performance, not metadata tricks: does the viewer click vs. ignore, do they keep watching (avg view duration + avg % viewed), do they like it. Good packaging gets the click; the video earns the rest. (answer/11914225)
  • "Made for Kids" = No is correct. YouTube states family-friendly content isn't automatically "made for kids," and "animated content that appeals to everyone" should be set not made for kids. (answer/9528076)
  • AI disclosure = No. Disclosure is required only for realistic content a viewer could mistake for real — clearly-animated cartoons are exempt. (answer/14328491)
  • Schedule = private auto-flipped to public; no algorithmic difference from a manual flip, and the public "Published on" date is the go-public moment. HD (1080p) can take hours to process while low quality serves first — the reason to upload early and wait. (answer/1270709 · /71674)
  • Tags are minor but not dead: YouTube still uses them for search relevance and to catch spelling variants (useful for Hindi/romanized spellings) — worth filling, not worth obsessing over. Hashtags: over 60 and YouTube ignores them all. (answer/16090438 · /6390658)

When to publish

📅 Friday, 10 July 2026 · 5:30 PM IST

Then a fixed rhythm: one video every 2 days, always at 5:30 PM IST (next: Sun 12 Jul → Tue 14 Jul → Thu 16 Jul…). After 10 videos are live, move to daily. Note: YouTube's own data shows upload frequency alone doesn't lift views — the reason to be consistent is different and real: a fixed slot builds viewer habit, and each upload is a fresh at-bat for the search index to test. More quality videos = more chances for one to catch. That's how every channel in our tracker grew.

Why 5:30 PM: we timestamped all 120 recent competitor uploads. 48% land between 4–7 PM IST — this is when the Hindi family/kids audience comes online after school. 6:00 PM is the single busiest hour (23 uploads). Publishing ~30 min ahead of it means our video is fully indexed and searchable when the evening wave arrives, instead of competing head-on with the big channels' 6:00 PM drops. This matches the independent research too: 5–8 PM local time (esp. Tue/Thu) is the top-performing slot for kids/family content.

How to publish — the exact flow

Answer to the team's question: upload early as Private, then let YouTube's Schedule flip it Public. Never upload-and-instantly-publish, and never flip visibility by hand. A scheduled video's "publish time" is the moment it goes public — uploading a day early costs nothing and fixes two real problems: processing and checks.

  1. 1
    Thursday 9 July, evening: upload the video with visibility Private. Fill every field from the upload sheet — title, description, tags, thumbnail, playlist, audience, end screen, card.
  2. 2
    Wait for processing to finish up to HD (1080p). A video published while only SD is ready shows a blurry stream to the first viewers — the worst first impression. Check: quality selector on the private watch page shows 1080p.
  3. 3
    Wait for Checks (copyright + ad suitability) to go green. This is the whole point of uploading early — problems surface while the video is still private.
  4. 4
    Set visibility to Schedule → Fri 10 July, 5:30 PM IST. YouTube flips it public automatically, on the second. Do not use Premiere — a Premiere on a 0-subscriber channel creates an empty countdown room and wastes the first hour.
  5. 5
    Friday 5:35 PM IST: open the live video, post + pin the pinned comment (block in First 48h), and like it from the channel account.

First 48 hours — do this, and only this

✅ Do

  • Pin the comment within 5 minutes of going live (block below), like it from the channel.
  • Reply + heart every real comment within the first day — it encourages more comments and keeps early viewers engaged.
  • Watch the video once fully, logged in on a phone (real watch, no repeats).
  • Share the link once per person to family/friends WhatsApp — ask them to watch to the end, not just click like.
  • Log day-1 numbers (impressions, CTR, avg view duration) from Studio → Analytics → Reach, in the team sheet.

🚫 Don't

  • No sub4sub, no bought views/likes — fake engagement is a strike-able policy violation and poisons the audience signal permanently.
  • Don't mass-blast the link in Telegram/Facebook link-exchange groups — junk clicks with 5-second watch times tell YouTube the video is bad.
  • Don't delete or re-upload video 1 — fix its metadata instead (next section).
  • Don't touch title/thumbnail for 7 days after publish — let the search index settle before judging.
  • Don't copy this description to the next video — every video gets fresh story copy (mass-produced sameness is what the July-2025 "inauthentic content" policy demonetizes).

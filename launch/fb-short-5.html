An ailing, wealthy turnip Seth trusts his poor caretaker, Lauki, with his cash box above all his own relatives. When the Seth faints and the money vanishes overnight, a greedy relative brands Lauki a thief and drives him out. But Lauki never stole a rupee — he rushed every coin to the hospital to pay for the old man's operation. When the Seth wakes, the receipt in Lauki's name tells the truth, and the caretaker is welcomed back as family.

Why a scroller stops: A poor caretaker is thrown out as a thief, a folded paper pressed to his chest, saying nothing to defend himself — and you stay to learn what is really on that paper.

Setup: the Seth trusts him with everything→The faint: strength fails, he rushes out→The missing money: a false alarm→The accusation: branded a thief→Dignity: he says nothing, walks away→The reveal: a paid-operation receipt→The turn: the Seth stops the mob→Payoff: welcomed back as family
Built for continuity — it should play as ONE unbroken film, not clips. Two things do that: (1) the story stays in a few connected sets and the camera flows between beats (walk / pan / push) instead of cutting — each scene's Flow line says how it joins the one before. (2) when you render, take the LAST frame of each finished clip and use it as the input / first image for the next clip (Flow → “Frames to Video” / Extend); every video prompt below also carries a continuity lock telling Veo to continue the previous shot with no cut.

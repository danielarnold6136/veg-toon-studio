A poor potato delivery boy squints at a cracked, spider-webbed phone while a rich pomegranate laughs at him and flicks a coin at his feet. Wounded but unbroken, he saves coin by coin, sets up one small cart on his corner and builds it, day and night, into his own thriving delivery shop — until the day the pomegranate's own company collapses and he comes to that same corner asking the potato for work.

Why a scroller stops: A delivery boy stares at a phone screen shattered into a spider-web, wipes one tear, and quietly says he'll build his own company one day — and you stay to watch it happen.

Setup: a rider and a cracked phone→Mockery: the rich man laughs→Resolve: one tear, then fire→The grind: one cart, day and night→Growth: the corner becomes a shop→Reversal: the rich man's firm falls→The turn: he offers his mocker work→Payoff: hard work, never small
Built for continuity — it should play as ONE unbroken film, not clips. Two things do that: (1) the story stays in a few connected sets and the camera flows between beats (walk / pan / push) instead of cutting — each scene's Flow line says how it joins the one before. (2) when you render, take the LAST frame of each finished clip and use it as the input / first image for the next clip (Flow → “Frames to Video” / Extend); every video prompt below also carries a continuity lock telling Veo to continue the previous shot with no cut.
Same reference model as Short 1: whole-vegetable head with NO human hair (the veg's own top is the crown) · a complete payoff arc · render the reference portraits first and reuse them as Flow Ingredients · every dialogue line spoken inside its own clip (🎤 one mouth moving at a time — no separate VO recording) · neutral/Hindu framing.

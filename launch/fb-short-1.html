A poor okra boy who runs a tiny roadside tea stall finds a wealthy man's fat wallet, stuffed with cash and a gold card, dropped in the dust. Hungry and broke, he could keep it — instead he runs it back. The rich man first suspects him of stealing, then counts every note, sees his mistake, and offers the honest boy a partnership. Months later the little stall is a busy café — because honesty was the one thing money couldn't buy.

Why a scroller stops: A ragged tea-boy stands holding a fat wallet bursting with cash, his own coin tin empty beside him — you stop to see whether he keeps it or gives it back.

Setup: an honest boy, ten rupees to his name→Inciting: a rich man drops a fat wallet→Temptation: cash in his hand, hunger in his eyes→The choice: he runs to return it→The test: accused of stealing→Dignity: he asks for nothing→The turn: shame becomes respect→Payoff: the stall becomes his café
Built for continuity — it should play as ONE unbroken film, not clips. Two things do that: (1) the story stays in a few connected sets and the camera flows between beats (walk / pan / push) instead of cutting — each scene's Flow line says how it joins the one before. (2) when you render, take the LAST frame of each finished clip and use it as the input / first image for the next clip (Flow → “Frames to Video” / Extend); every video prompt below also carries a continuity lock telling Veo to continue the previous shot with no cut.
Same reference model as Short 1: whole-vegetable head with NO human hair (the veg's own top is the crown) · a complete payoff arc · render the reference portraits first and reuse them as Flow Ingredients · one lip-synced line per clip (🎤) + the rest on a VO track · neutral/Hindu framing.

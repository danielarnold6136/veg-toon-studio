Facebook & Instagram — the full launch

Every fact below is marked by how well it is proven. OFFICIAL means it comes from Meta's own Terms, Help Centre, Transparency Center or newsroom. SECONDARY means strong industry consensus that Meta does not publish. UNVERIFIED means do not rely on it. MEASURED means we pulled it from Meta ourselves today.

Anything not marked OFFICIAL should be confirmed in the app before you depend on it.

Your question: new ID, or your existing one?

Use your existing personal profile — as the invisible admin of a brand-new Page. Do not create a new personal Facebook profile.

You were right to be uneasy, but the risk runs the opposite way from what you assumed. A second personal profile is the dangerous option, not the safe one — and it buys you no privacy, because the admin behind a Facebook Page is never shown to the public. Visitors see “Veggie Toons TV”. They cannot see you.

Why a new personal Facebook profile is the wrong move

Registering a profile called “Veggie Toons” breaks three separate Meta rules at once.

Facebook Terms of Service, §3.1 (effective 1 January 2025) OFFICIAL
“Provide for your account the same name that you use in everyday life.” … “Create only one account (your own) and use it for personal purposes.”
Community Standards — Authentic Identity Representation OFFICIAL
Meta “will seek further information before taking actions ranging from temporarily restricting to permanently disabling profiles or accounts” if you “create an account that represents a non-human entity, such as a business, pet, or fictional character,” or “create or maintain multiple Facebook accounts.”
Community Standards — Account Integrity (updated 28 May 2026) OFFICIAL
Meta may disable accounts “Owned by the same person or entity as an account that has been disabled.”

Read those together and the burner-profile plan collapses. A brand-named profile is a policy violation from the moment it exists. If it gets caught, it triggers a name-review checkpoint — government ID, sometimes a video selfie. And because Meta reserves the right to act on accounts owned by the same person as a disabled account, a burner does not quarantine risk from your real profile. It links the two and can take both down. Enforcement escalates over five strikes.

Also not a workaround: Meta's “additional profiles”.

Since 21 September 2023 an eligible adult may add up to four additional personal profiles, and these are allowed to use a name other than your legal one OFFICIAL. But they are useless here: at launch they excluded Professional Mode and payments, their policies forbid you to “falsely represent that you are authorized to speak on behalf of another person, brand, business or organization,” and they sit underneath your one account — so they insulate nothing. They are for a second personal identity, not for a business.

Instagram is the opposite — it genuinely needs nothing of yours

  • An Instagram account can be created from scratch with only an email address or phone number, with no Facebook profile anywhere in the picture OFFICIAL. Meta now supports separate Meta Accounts per app, and Accounts Center links your identities only if you choose to.
  • So register a brand-new Instagram on a brand-new brand email. It is never connected to you personally.
  • The single point where they must touch: to cross-post between the Facebook Page and Instagram, the Instagram account must be switched to Professional (a personal Instagram account cannot connect to a Page), and a personal Facebook profile with admin rights sits behind the Page OFFICIAL. That connection lives inside your Business Portfolio. It is not shown publicly.

So: Page or ID?

A Page. There is no “first name / last name” on a Facebook Page — that pair of fields only exists on a personal profile, and we are not making one. A Page has a single Page name. On Instagram there is likewise no first/last split: only a username and a display name.

The structure to build

1

Your existing personal Facebook profile, real name, left exactly as it is. It never appears on the Page. Make sure it is in good standing first — a profile with recent violations cannot create additional profiles and attracts scrutiny.

2

From it, create the “Veggie Toons TV” Facebook Page. This is the public brand and the only thing viewers see.

3

Create a Meta Business Portfolio (formerly Business Manager) and put the Page inside it. This is what lets you hand access to someone later without ever sharing your personal login.

4

Separately, register the Instagram account with a new brand email and phone. Switch it to a Professional account. Creator and Business unlock the identical monetization tools — the choice does not gate any earnings OFFICIAL.

5

Add the Instagram account to the same Business Portfolio and link it to the Page. Now cross-posting works, and your personal identity stays private.

One honest caveat. Keeping the brand on a separate email and out of your personal Accounts Center reduces public cross-linking. It does not, and must not try to, hide same-person ownership from Meta — deliberately structuring accounts to evade enforcement is itself a violation under the Account Integrity policy OFFICIAL. Running a brand Page from the same device and IP as your personal profile is completely normal and allowed, as long as your profile is in good standing.

The format rules — these are not style choices

Length

  • Facebook has no length cap. Since Meta's 17 June 2025 change, all Facebook videos are reels and organic uploads have no length or format limit OFFICIAL. Post the full 5–7 minute story natively to Facebook.
  • Instagram Reels officially allow 20 minutes — Instagram's Help Centre states “You can record and edit videos up to 20 minutes with Instagram Reels.” OFFICIAL But the same page says “Reels over 3 minutes won't be recommended to new audiences.”
  • For a 0-follower account, being recommended to non-followers is the only distribution that exists. Therefore every Instagram Reel must be 3:00 or shorter. No exceptions. Cut the story, or split it into parts.

Watermarks — the trap

Adam Mosseri, Head of Instagram, 11 March 2025 OFFICIAL
“We don't penalize content for being created in other apps. Just make sure you avoid posting content with watermarks, as that can affect your distribution.”
  • Instagram makes a reel less visible if it is low-resolution, watermarked, muted, bordered, majority-text, or already-posted OFFICIAL. Facebook's guidance: “Avoid using visible third party watermarks and content that is visibly recycled from other apps or sources.”
  • The signal is other-platform provenance, not the presence of a logo. Your own Veggie Toons badge is fine. A YouTube or CapCut mark is not.
  • This makes our animated logo watermark load-bearing: the Veo 3 render mark in the bottom-right corner is a third-party watermark. It must be covered on every clip we post to Meta.
  • Never letterbox. Cropping a 16:9 story into 9:16 with black bars is a “bordered” reel and is demoted by name. Reframe properly, or don't post it vertically.

Originality — the thing that could kill the channel

Facebook's unoriginal-content policy launched 14 July 2025 and was reinforced on 13 March 2026. It penalizes re-uploading content you had no role in creating, and “low-value” edits — adding borders, inserting captions, changing reel speed. Repeat offenders are deprioritized, then made non-recommendable (invisible to non-followers), then demonetized, with reduced distribution “on everything they share.”

Meta for Creators, “Combating unoriginal content” OFFICIAL
“You can share the same content across any of your Pages or profiles.”
  • We are safe on ownership. We produce every frame. Re-cutting our own YouTube video into a Facebook or Instagram upload is a transformation of content we made, not reuse of someone else's.
  • Ownership is necessary but not sufficient. Three things still bite regardless of who owns the video: a visible third-party watermark, a lazy edit that adds nothing (borders, captions, speed), and duplicate-copy detection routing views to the original.
  • On Instagram, Mosseri put it plainly on 30 April 2026: “If most of what you post to Instagram is someone else's content, your account is no longer going to be recommendable.” OFFICIAL
  • Meta reports original-reel views and watch time on Facebook approximately doubled in the second half of 2025 OFFICIAL. Original work is being actively rewarded.
  • AI animation is not the problem. Bagu Baba TV advertises “AI animated” in its Page bio and runs a healthy Page. Meta penalizes unoriginal, not synthetic.
Ignore the numbers you'll read on blogs.

“40–60% more distribution”, “60–70% less reach with a watermark”, “10+ reposts in 30 days” — none of these appear in any Meta document. Meta frames the threshold qualitatively. Do not plan around invented figures.

The launch

How a 0-follower account gets seen at all

  • Both Reels surfaces serve non-followers by default. Facebook's own Transparency Center says the Reels candidate set includes “content you may be interested in from creators you may not follow”, and Mosseri states that on Reels “the majority of what you see is from accounts you don't follow” OFFICIAL. You do not need followers to be shown. You need to be watched.
  • Instagram picks roughly 100 candidate reels and ranks them on watch time (a 3-second threshold), likes, saves, comments, reshares, audio clicks, reel length and skip behaviour. The follower signal it uses is how many follows the reel itself produced — not how many you already have OFFICIAL. A new account is not handicapped.
  • There is no Meta source claiming Facebook Reels gives new pages more reach than Instagram. One third-party study (12 Aug 2025) found Instagram reels averaged more views and roughly five times the engagement rate of Facebook's. Directional only — but it does not support the common “Facebook is easier” claim. SECONDARY
  • Professional Mode on a profile has no documented reach advantage over a Page OFFICIAL. Choose the Page, for monetization.

What to post

1

Facebook, the full story. Upload the complete 5–7 minute video natively, horizontal, in one piece. No length cap, and this is the surface that can pay. Never upload the YouTube file — export a clean master with the Veo 3 corner mark covered by our badge.

2

Instagram, a 3-minute cut. Hard ceiling. A properly reframed vertical cut, not a letterboxed crop — the story's climax, ending on the moral. Point the bio link at the full YouTube video.

3

Both, a 30–45 second hook. The betrayal beat alone, cut to end on the unanswered question. This is the piece that a non-follower actually watches to the end, and completion is the strongest ranking signal on both surfaces.

4

Every clip is a fresh export. Never re-upload a file that has already been posted elsewhere — Instagram lists “already-posted” among the things that make a reel less visible. Re-render, don't recycle.

Before you post anything: the Veo 3 render mark sits in the bottom-right of every clip. On Meta that is a third-party watermark and it will cost you distribution. The animated badge on the watermark page exists to cover it. Nothing goes to Facebook or Instagram until that mark is gone.

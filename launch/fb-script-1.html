At a glittering family reception the wealthy bahu is settled onto a velvet sofa dripping with gold while the poor bahu, finding no seat, quietly sits on the floor and keeps serving. Everyone waits for the family's most revered elder to arrive and bless the golden sofa — but she walks straight past the gold, sits down on the floor beside the overlooked bahu, and begins to reveal who this girl truly is… and the story cuts.

Why a scroller stops: The very first frame is one bride on a velvet sofa buried in gold and the other on the bare floor with a serving tray — you already know who is about to be overlooked, and you stay to see if anyone will notice her.

Setup: status seated by gold→Exclusion: no seat, so the floor→Contrast: gold vs the mat→Kindness: her husband joins her on the floor→Expectation: the great elder arrives→The turn: she chooses the floor→Shock: the room breaks→Cliffhanger: who IS this girl? — cut
How this rebuild fixes the three problems — read once before you render:
① Story. This is now a real 8-beat arc (see the chips above), not a set of moods — it builds to a genuine turn and cuts on an unfinished reveal, so people have to comment for Part 2.
② Character continuity. Text alone can never keep a face the same across separate renders. So FIRST render the 5 reference portraits below, approve them, then load the ones you need as Flow “Ingredients” / image references in every scene. Each scene now names only the characters actually in it (never all five), so the model stops reinventing outfits.
③ Dialogue. Every clip has exactly one lip-synced line (only that character’s mouth moves in Veo — it can’t mix speakers up). Every other line is on a VO track you record and lay in during the edit. The 🎤 line goes in the Veo prompt; the VO lines are listed separately per scene.

① Reference portraits — render these FIRST, approve, then reuse as Flow Ingredients

Do this before any scene. Render one clean portrait per character, pick the best, and keep it. In every scene prompt below you then load the needed portraits (up to 3) as image references so the face, head-vegetable and outfit stay identical across the whole part. This is the single fix for “different outfits every image”.

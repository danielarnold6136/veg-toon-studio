A son builds a new pakka house and finds ‘a little less room’ in it for his own father. One rainy night his daughter carries her blanket across the courtyard to the leaking mud house — and by morning the cot comes back across the courtyard, and the biggest room in the new house is his.

Why a scroller stops: A father carries his own bedding out of his son's housewarming. Everything after that is one rainy night deciding, drip by drip, which house really has the bigger room.

Rupture: no room in the new house→A lantern from next door→Rain finds the old roof — no words→A blanket crosses the courtyard→An umbrella at the door→The cot comes back→Moral part 1: the hero to camera→Moral part 2: narrator over both houses
This set (16–18) — the same locks as 14–15, three new angles, one scene shorter. Everything measured off the 2M-view reel (teardown §09) stays law here: (1) vegetables all the way down — every piece of exposed skin is the character’s own vegetable colour and material, no human skin anywhere; (2) deep, sharp, fully-built REAL sets — the empty blurred backdrop is banned in every prompt; (3) visually-led delivery — 14–15 total lines across 56 seconds with one genuinely silent beat per film; (4) locked-off tripod cameras with exactly two eased moves per film; (5) hybrid cutting — flow inside the same place and minute, HARD CUT across any change of place or time; (6) the two-part moral — the hero turns to camera for the first half, and a separate, lower narrator closes over a picture-only final shot. What CHANGES: the envelope drops from 9 scenes to 8 scenes / 56s, the register moves to the village — courtyards, bunds, wells, a pond, a peepal — and the three films test three different engines against each other: 16 = family, 17 = greed-karma, and 18 = the first out-and-out COMEDY in the series. Same repertory five as Shorts 14–15 — reuse the approved portraits, do not re-render them.
Cuts — hybrid, exactly like 14–15. Shots inside the same place and the same minute flow as one unbroken take (take the LAST frame of the clip as the first image of the next, via Flow → “Frames to Video” / Extend), and every change of place or time is a clean HARD CUT — no dissolves, no fades, ever. Each scene below is marked either Flow → or HARD CUT and its video prompt is written accordingly. Short 16 has 6 hard cuts; Shorts 17 and 18 have 5 each.
Lock the camera off. Three of the reference reel’s five shots have literally zero camera movement — no pan, no drift, no zoom — held for a full 10 seconds, which AI video models essentially never do by themselves. That stillness is a large part of why it reads as a film rather than as AI footage. Every scene below is therefore a locked-off static tripod shot, except two deliberate eased moves per film (marked on those scenes). If a clip comes back drifting or breathing, re-render it.
The two-part moral — the reference’s signature, kept. Scene X.7 is the hero to camera, lip-synced in-clip, addressing the viewer as दोस्तो. Scene X.8 is picture-only: its two lines are a separate NARRATOR voice you record and lay on in the edit, in a distinctly lower, calmer voice than any character — and they are deliberately kept out of the Veo prompt so Veo cannot try to lip-sync them. The comedy in Short 18 keeps the construction and simply lets the moral smile.

Veo 3 clip lengths
Why nothing rendered before, and what changed. Every old script leaned on cruelty — dowry demands, threats, “keep your head down” — which is exactly what Veo 3 refuses. These ten carry the same emotional punch through situation (exclusion, being overlooked, a kindness repaid, a reveal) instead of abuse, so the clips actually generate. Every line, every action and every prompt is swept against a banned-token list before it ships.
Two prompts per shot, both paragraphs. ① the image prompt paints one keyframe and re-states every character’s full vegetable-head design from scratch (that is what stops the vegetable drifting between shots); ② the image-to-video prompt animates that keyframe for 4, 6 or 8 seconds with the Hindi dialogue lip-synced. Generate the image first, approve it, then feed image + video prompt into Veo 3.
If one heavy clip still gets refused, render it silent (delete the dialogue sentence from the video prompt) and lay that same line in as a voice track in the edit — the winning channels do exactly this. And two things always go in post, never in Veo: the PART 1 / COMMENT / TAG overlays (AI Devanagari breaks), and the badge covering the bottom-right corner to hide any generator watermark.

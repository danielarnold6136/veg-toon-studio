A big farmer turns the shared channel into his own field at night and lets a small farmer's seedlings crack. The village answers with a line of pots from the well. Then the water he hoarded swamps his own crop — and the man he dried out is the one who wades in with a spade to save it.

Why a scroller stops: A man turns the whole village channel into his own field at night and laughs off one warning on the bund. Two mornings later the only thing his money is holding is a spade, knee-deep in his own mirror.

Rupture: the water is turned at night→Cracked earth by morning→The pot line — no words→A warning, laughed off→His field is a pond→The same spade opens the drain→Moral part 1: the hero to camera→Moral part 2: narrator over both fields
